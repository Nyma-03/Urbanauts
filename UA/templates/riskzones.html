The system will also include risk zone detection to guide safer and more sustainable city planning. For instance, Flood Risk Zones will be identified using data and prototype images to alert both citizens and planners. These areas should not be used for housing but can instead be reserved for wetlands, parks, or fish farming, ensuring timely evacuation of citizens when needed. Similarly, Rainfall Risk Zones will be highlighted to show regions of consistently high rainfall. This information will help citizens and developers avoid designating these areas for farming that requires ample sunlight or for sensitive infrastructure, thereby ensuring both productivity and safety. Lastly, Drought-Prone Zones will be mapped, as these areas are unsuitable for farming. Instead, they can be better utilized for solar power plants or other purposes more aligned with their climate conditions. By providing these visual risk assessments, users—both citizens and city planners—will be able to understand the environmental challenges of their area and make informed decisions after selecting their city.
